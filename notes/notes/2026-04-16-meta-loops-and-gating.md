# Meta-Loops and Gating — Crystallization as Nested Processing

Working note from the 2026-04-16 session. Extends the 2026-04-14 three-layer architecture (`2026-04-14-stage-landscape-loop.md`) after the diffuse-to-focused crystallization question was pushed on and broke open into something more nested than the earlier picture had assumed. This note is the architectural reference; the journal entry is the narrative.

## The starting point

Coming out of 2026-04-14 we had: stage (fast, perception-colored-by-memory), landscape (slow, consolidated terrain), loop (activity across both). Prediction reclassified as one mode of the loop among five (prediction, recall, imagination, reasoning, creativity), distinguished by question shape.

Open question #2 was: *what makes a diffuse-mode item salient enough to crystallize into a focused-mode question?* Picked as the pull for this session because the three-layer split had just settled and the crystallization seam was the place to test whether the split held.

## What changed

The one-step crystallization picture — snag appears → question forms → loop runs — does not match the phenomenology. When Dylan described what actually happens to him on encountering a contradiction in a paper, two prior steps surfaced that the one-step model skipped.

### Self-check comes first

Before a snag gets treated as a real question, it gets tested for whether it's even a snag. First move is "did I miss something / misread / misinterpret?" The snag has to survive an encoding-check before being promoted.

Confirmed structural (not genre-specific): the same move fires in friend conversations — "wait, didn't you say X earlier?" is the replay-request version of self-check. So the pattern isn't research-training; it's built into how snags work.

Why it has to be built in: a raw snag is ambiguous between *I misencoded something* and *the world is actually incoherent here*. Both produce identical-feeling snags — two stage items failing to cohere against the landscape, regardless of cause. You can't tell which one you have just from the felt snag. Self-check always runs first to disambiguate.

### Salience gates second

After the snag survives self-check and is confirmed as an object-level contradiction, there's a separate call: does this matter enough to pursue? Most confirmed contradictions get noted, deferred, or dropped rather than chased.

Salience is landscape-weighted by context. Research-paper mode: moderate-to-high salience, contradictions matter. News-article mode: low salience, contradictions expected and not worth chasing. Friend-conversation mode: depends on who.

## The revised picture

### Three nested layers before object-level processing

1. **Raw snag.** Stage items fail to cohere against landscape. Pre-verbal. Ambiguous between encoding failure and real incoherence.
2. **Meta-loop: self-check.** Loop runs on "is the snag real?" Tests encoding, replays input, checks priors. Either dissolves or promotes.
3. **Salience gate.** Loop runs on "is the real snag worth pursuing?" Landscape-weighted by context. Either parks or commits.
4. **Object-level processing.** Only reached if the snag survives all three prior layers. This is what the 2026-04-14 note was calling "crystallization." It is the end of a longer pipeline, not a single event.

### The loop serves multiple question types in sequence

Object-level processing — the loop running on a question about the world — is the third and narrowest use of the loop. The prior two uses are the loop running on questions *about the loop's own activity*:

- Self-check is the loop asking "was my read of the stage correct?"
- Salience-gating is the loop asking "should I commit processing-time to this object-level run?"

This reframes what the loop is doing most of the time. Most loop-activity is metacognitive bookkeeping, not world-modeling.

### Landscape must be finer-grained than previously assumed

Dylan's observation: who is speaking dials self-check and salience differently for the same snag. A snag from a careful friend: self-check skews "I probably misheard." A snag from a friend who's loose with facts: self-check skews "they probably misspoke." Same snag, same genre, different dial settings.

That means the landscape carries **per-source models granular enough to condition the meta-loops**, not just the object-level loop. Not a flat terrain of grooves. A structured representation of specific people, specific domains, specific sources — with those models accessible to whatever phase of loop activity needs them.

### Layers are nested but not independent

When the source is trusted, self-check and salience read from overlapping priors and tend to converge ("probably I misheard; if real, probably worth pursuing"). When the source is modeled as unreliable, the phases split: self-check resolves fast in your favor ("probably they misspoke"), salience independently dials down ("but tracking their inconsistencies isn't worth it"). So the nesting is real but the layers share landscape structure and interact.

## What this says about the architecture

- The loop is a **multi-question instrument**. Same mechanism runs on snag-reality questions, salience questions, and world questions. Distinguished by what the question is about, not by having separate machinery.
- The landscape is **doing more work than the 2026-04-14 picture credited it with**. It colors the stage (established 2026-04-14), acts as the metric that determines when things don't cohere (my proposal, partially supported), and carries the per-source priors that tune how the meta-loops process their own outputs (this session).
- Crystallization is now **a survival-through-gates event**, not a single moment. The "moment of noticing" something is the completion of a multi-step process that may have been running for some time before becoming reportable.

## Floated, not committed

### Failure-mode predictions

If the architecture is right, gate failures should produce specific dysfunction patterns:

- Salience gate fails open (everything is salient) → mania-adjacent / psychosis-adjacent: every snag pursued, attention can't settle
- Self-check gate fails closed (no snag survives) → chronic doubt / dissociation: nothing feels real enough to commit to

This is not a claim that the architecture explains these clinical pictures. It is a claim that *if* the architecture is right, it would predict gate-failure profiles roughly matching those patterns. Not load-bearing. A possible firm-up test if the theory ever needs one.

### Consciousness as gatekeeper

Possible candidate identification: the subjective feel of "noticing" = the moment a snag survives the gates and becomes committed for object-level processing. Consciousness as the gatekeeping function, not a spotlight on content.

Attractive because it explains why noticing feels active rather than passive, and gives consciousness a specific computational job. Worries:

- Timing may not match — self-check is subsecond; does it precede consciousness or happen within it? Only works if there's pre-conscious snag-detection the gates admit into awareness.
- Narrows "consciousness" to a specific function. Diffuse-mode wandering is still conscious but doesn't involve snag-gating — would need a separate story.
- Doesn't touch why the gatekeeper has a felt quality at all (hard problem stays untouched).

On a shelf as *"if this architecture is right, here's what it would say about consciousness"* — not "this architecture explains consciousness." The difference matters.

## Open questions after this session

1. **What sets the dials on the gates during consolidation?** Structural answer to "what promotes a snag to a focused question" is now: survival through self-check and salience. But the mechanics of how per-source priors get *written* into the landscape — how the model of "this friend is careful" or "this journal is reliable" forms — is untouched.
2. **Anti-collapse as general principle** (carryover from 2026-04-14).
3. **JEPA-shaped landscape** (carryover from 2026-04-14).
4. **Residual timescale** — loop reads vs. consolidation writes. Still open. Today's work doesn't address it; my earlier framing that it did was overclaiming.
5. **Is there a fourth layer below self-check?** The raw snag has to come from somewhere. Is there a sub-threshold stage-landscape interaction that produces snags at all, and if so, what's its structure? Possibly what "intuition" or "pre-attention" names.

## What this note is not

Not a build spec. Not load-bearing. If the next session's thinking contradicts something here, the new thinking wins. This is scaffolding for where the architecture sits at the end of 2026-04-16, not a commitment.
